The Design Challenge

Improve clarity, trust and flow in a player-facing shop experience

A shop flow needs to be easy to understand, trustworthy, efficient and aligned with player expectations. The work required balancing ideal player experience with live-game constraints, established UI patterns, platform differences and technical implementation realities.

Player clarity

Can players quickly understand what is available, what each option means and what action they are about to take?

Low-friction flow

Is the purchase journey clear, efficient and free from unnecessary hesitation or confusion?

Technical feasibility

How can design improvements work within existing technical, mobile, PC and live-game constraints?

Design Decisions

Principles that shaped the work

01 - Reduce friction
Make the journey straightforward.

The player journey should have fewer unclear steps, stronger signposting and a clear path from intent to action.

02 - Improve clarity
Make content understandable at a glance.

Shop content, states, options and purchase steps needed to communicate clearly without relying on guesswork.

03 - Respect platform differences
Design for PC and mobile contexts.

UI and flow decisions needed to consider different inputs, screen spaces, expectations and implementation constraints.

04 - Use evidence
Let data challenge assumptions.

Telemetry and player metrics helped move the conversation from what we thought might be happening to what the data suggested needed attention.
