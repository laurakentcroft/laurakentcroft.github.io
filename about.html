About Me

Game Designer, creative thinker and former programmer

I’m a Game Designer with a background in programming, currently focused on gameplay, systems, technical design and player experience. I enjoy working on features that sit between creative design and practical implementation; turning ideas into systems that are clear, achievable and satisfying for players.

I’m especially interested in gameplay systems, quests, narrative, progression, player motivation and the small usability details that make games feel intuitive.

I like designing practical, player-focused systems that make sense creatively and technically.

My programming background helps me understand implementation constraints, communicate clearly with engineers and design features that are more likely to survive contact with production.

My Journey

My Journey Into Game Design

Where it started

It all began as a child when I got my first games on the Sega Mega Drive. Since then, I've had a real enthusiasm for games and wanted to understand how things worked under the hood.

How I moved into development

I started by joining the School of Code bootcamp and learning full stack development, whilst teaching myself C++ and C# in the evenings.

That programming background helps me design with feasibility in mind. I enjoy working across the full life of a feature, from early ideas, research and references through to documentation, prototyping, implementation, balancing, testing, feedback and iteration.

Design Approach

How I Approach Design

01

Start with the player

My design approach is practical, collaborative and player-focused. I try to start with the player experience: what should the player understand, feel, choose or achieve?

02

Understand the system

From there, I think about how the system works, how it connects to the wider game, what constraints the team is working within, and how the feature can be communicated clearly.

03

Keep it achievable

I care about making designs that are creative, understandable and achievable for the teams building them.

Inspiration

What Inspires My Work

Game design inspiration

I’m inspired by games that combine strong mechanics with memorable worlds, characters and player choices. I’m especially drawn to design that feels invisible when it works well: clear feedback, satisfying progression, readable systems and moments where players instinctively understand what they can do next.

Outside of games

Outside of games, I’m a tech enthusiast, sports fan, wildlife lover, gardener and proud owner of two mischievous cats. I like bringing curiosity from different parts of life into the way I think about players, systems and worlds.
